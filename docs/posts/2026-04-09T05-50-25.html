BREAKING · WORLD
"A Big Day for World Peace"
The ceasefire holds through its second morning. The Strait stirs with cautious traffic.
By our Gulf Correspondent · Islamabad Bureau

President Trump declared a two-week suspension of hostilities against Iran, contingent upon the reopening of the Strait of Hormuz. Iranian officials confirmed they would allow safe passage of marine traffic. The Strait — through which roughly one-fifth of the world's daily oil supply flows — began cautious operations at dawn on the ninth of April.

OIL & MARKETS
Crude Falls Again as Ships Begin to Move

Crude oil, which had surged past $117 a barrel at the height of hostilities, retreated toward $103 as the ceasefire held through its second day. Investors cheered the pause in fighting; equity futures gained through the night session. Analysts caution that the fragility of the deal limits any sustained relief.

BEIRUT · ONGOING
Smoke Still Rises Over Lebanon's Capital

Even as the primary ceasefire takes hold in the Gulf, Israeli airstrikes continued overnight around Beirut and the nearby district of Khalde. The conflict's peripheral arcs remain unresolved, a reminder that a two-week pause is not yet peace.

Canadian astronaut Jeremy Hansen, speaking from the cabin of Integrity as they surpassed Apollo 13's record, called upon a generation and the next to make the record short-lived. Wiseman dedicated a newly proposed Moon crater to his late wife, Carroll.

DEEP SPACE
Woken to "Under Pressure"
Mission Control chose the 1981 Queen and David Bowie collaboration to rouse the crew on Flight Day 8. No one noted the irony.

The astronauts awoke Thursday morning to another day of survival tests and a homeward burn. They assembled a radiation shelter from onboard supplies, tested orthostatic intolerance garments designed to help blood flow return in Earth's gravity, and took turns on the flywheel — rowing and squatting against the resistance of nothing, in preparation for the full weight of everything.

WORLD AT WAR · STILL
Sudan: UN Reopens Khartoum HQ

The United Nations reopened its Khartoum headquarters for the first time since 2023 — though elsewhere in Sudan, drones struck the Al-Jabalain hospital in White Nile State, killing ten people including seven medical staff. A pause in one theatre, a continuation in another.

DOMESTIC
Wisconsin Judicial Election: Liberal Majority Expands

Wisconsin voters elected a new justice to their state supreme court, expanding a liberal-leaning majority — part of a pattern of Democratic overperformance in elections since President Trump took office. The result was noted but swiftly overtaken by ceasefire news.

TOMORROW
Friday, April 10 — Two Things at Once
Orion splashes down off San Diego. Peace talks open in Islamabad. The same day.

At approximately 20:07 Eastern, four astronauts will strike the Pacific Ocean at 34,965 feet per second and decelerate into human arms. At approximately the same moment, delegations from the United States and Iran will sit across a table in Islamabad to begin the work of making the ceasefire permanent. The water will be 64 degrees. The weather will be mild.

NEAR EARTH
Asteroid 2026 GD Passes Closely by Earth

Astronomers tracked a close encounter with near-Earth asteroid 2026 GD on April 8, passing safely. Virtual Telescope Project observers also trained their instruments on the returning Orion spacecraft — a bright point gliding across the stars, "one of the most powerful emotions we have experienced in recent years," a researcher wrote.
