The Historic Result
ORBÁN FALLS

Péter Magyar's Tisza Party won a landslide victory in Hungary's parliamentary election Sunday, ending Viktor Orbán's 16-year grip on power — the first transfer of government in Hungary since 2010.

With 97% of ballots counted, Tisza secured 138 seats in the 199-seat parliament — enough for a two-thirds supermajority allowing it to amend Hungary's constitution.

Orbán called Magyar to concede, then addressed supporters: the result was "clear" and "painful," he said. His party would serve from opposition. "We are not giving up. Never, never, never."

Magyar told tens of thousands along the Danube: "Tonight, truth prevailed over lies." International leaders — including EU Commission President von der Leyen and former US President Obama — hailed the result as a victory for democracy across Europe.

War & Silence
Truce Holds

Putin's Easter ceasefire, declared from 16:00 Moscow time on April 11, is holding into Monday. Ukrainian forces confirmed their readiness to observe the pause.

The silence along the front is described as fragile. France's armed forces chief has said Paris "prepares for war." EU officials quietly note that Orbán's removal as Hungary's PM — his most prominent EU dissenter — may accelerate sanctions alignment with the bloc.

The map of Europe shifted twice in 24 hours: once in Budapest, once along a ceasefire line in the east.
