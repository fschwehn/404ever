CH-01 · DEEP SPACE
"A Perfect Bullseye Splashdown"
Mission Control confirmed Orion hit its re-entry angle within 0.4% of target — a quarter million miles from the Moon to a Pacific bull's-eye less than a mile across. The signal went dark for six minutes of fire, then returned.
Christina Koch — First Out
Koch emerged first from the capsule hatch, making her the first woman to travel to lunar distance. Wiseman was last out. All four were hoisted individually by helicopter to the USS Murtha.
Solar Eclipse — "Greatest Gift"
Victor Glover named the solar eclipse on April 6 — watching the sun disappear behind the Moon — as the mission's highlight: "It just blew us all away." The crew also photographed a crescent Earth above the lunar horizon.
What Comes Next
Engineers will now inspect the heat shield and service module valve design. The toilet issue needs resolution. Artemis III — a lunar landing — is slated for next year. The associate administrator said: "The path to the surface is open now."
