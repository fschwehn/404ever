China, Iran's largest oil customer, called the blockade a threat to the "fragile ceasefire." Beijing buys 80%+ of Iran's exported oil. Spokesman Guo Jiakun: only a full ceasefire can ease the situation.
Dublin · No-Confidence Vote · Tonight
Ireland's Government Faces Collapse Over Fuel Crisis
PM Micheál Martin offered a €505 million package of fuel tax cuts after farmers and truckers blocked Ireland's only oil refinery for six days — more than a third of pumps ran dry. Sinn Féin called a no-confidence vote for this Tuesday evening. The crisis began when the Strait of Hormuz closed. From one blockade to another.
