DUBLIN · DÁIL ÉIREANN
GOVT SURVIVES
Ireland: Government Wins Confidence Vote 92–78
The Fianna Fáil–Fine Gael coalition survived a Sinn Féin no-confidence motion — but not without drama. Junior Minister Michael Healy-Rae resigned mid-debate and voted against the government, calling it the "first significant crack in Coalition unity." The coalition's majority now rests on just five seats above the threshold.
The crisis began when the Strait of Hormuz closed — Ireland's fuel prices exceeded €2.34/litre (over $8.59/gallon). Farmers and truckers blocked the sole oil refinery at Whitegate, County Cork for six days. A €505m package — 10c/litre excise cut on petrol and diesel — narrowly quieted the Dáil. From one blockade to another.
