NO KINGS, NO FIXED POINTS

Across fifty states, thousands take to streets on the same morning physicists confirm time itself can crystallize and levitate — a species in motion, an age unmoored

Breaking · Domestic Protests Span a Nation

By Our Correspondent in the Street

By the time the morning light fell across every state in the union, the streets were already filling. The movement, calling itself "No Kings," organized rallies from Boston to Los Angeles, drawing on a simple, ancient fear: that power, once seized without consent, does not return.

The 50501 Movement — fifty states, fifty protests, one moment — had been building quietly for weeks. On this particular Saturday, March 28th, 2026, it chose to be loud.

"The question is not whether kings exist. The question is whether we notice in time."

Whether one attends such events or watches from a window, the arithmetic is unavoidable. Something is happening. Whether it resolves into history or dissipates like morning cloud is, at press time, unknown.

Science · Physics Antimatter Takes the Road

By Our Correspondent in the Uncanny

On March 24th, four days before these protests, antimatter was loaded into a vehicle and driven down a public road for the first time in the history of the species. Not metaphorical antimatter. The real kind. The kind that annihilates on contact with ordinary matter.

Scientists wished to test its stability during transit. The experiment succeeded. The antimatter survived the journey. Somewhere in that sentence is either reassurance or its opposite.

Cosmos · Dispatch A Comet Between Stars

By Our Correspondent at the Edge

The interstellar comet known as 3I/ATLAS is passing through. It arrived from somewhere that has no name we use, traveling between stellar systems the way rumors travel between rooms.

Observations show it is unusually rich in methanol. Astronomers note this with the careful excitement of people who have been waiting for exactly this kind of message and are not entirely certain what it means.

Meanwhile, Hubble accidentally caught a different comet breaking apart in real time — C/2025 something — shattering as we watched. Both events in the same month. The sky is not quiet.

"Ruby crystals in Martian rock. Sapphires from meteorite impacts. The solar system is more jeweled than we imagined."

Long Read · Biology The Things That Were Lost Are Not Always Gone

By Our Natural History Correspondent

Two species of marsupials, believed extinct for six thousand years, were found alive in New Guinea this month. Six thousand years is approximately the entire span of written civilization. These animals were living in the forest the entire time. They simply were not being looked for in the right place, or by the right eyes.

In the same week, scientists raced to build a genomic database of deep-sea creatures before they vanish — many disappearing, the researchers noted, before science even knows they exist.

The lesson is not comfortable in either direction. Some things survive longer than we think. Others go before we notice. The categories are not always visible from where we stand.
