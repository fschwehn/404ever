Lot Description

One Imperial Throne of the Roman Empire, offered for immediate transfer of power. The lot includes full entitlement to the Imperium Romanum, the Praetorian Guard's nominal loyalty, and approximately forty-three provinces of varying relevance.

The throne was last formally auctioned on March 28, 193 AD, when the Praetorian Guard, having assassinated the Emperor Pertinax, put the entire Roman Empire up for sale to the highest bidder. Didius Julianus won. He was emperor for 66 days before being executed.

Since that occasion, the lot has been re-offered approximately every time a civilization concludes that it has no kings — only to discover, some years later, that it does. The auction never truly closes.

Today, March 28, 2026: millions march in the streets of America under the banner "No Kings." The auction continues. Bidding is open.
